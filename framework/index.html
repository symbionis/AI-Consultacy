The 5 Levels of AI Fluency.

Five levels of fluency. Each more advanced than the last — and the right one depends on the work in front of you.

AI gets pitched as transformation. Most of what people actually want from it is quieter: a way to do the same work with less mental weight, and more room for what matters.

Most people use AI at one level for some kinds of work, and another for others. That's normal — the right level depends on what you're trying to do. But the further you go, the more these tools become part of how you actually think.

The five levels

Each builds on what came before.

Read them in order. The further you go, the more these tools shape how you work.

L1
Better questions
Use AI instead of search when the answer needs judgment.
For anything where the right answer depends on context, comparison, or your particular situation — not facts that can be looked up, but questions that need to be thought through.
L2
Less weight
Take some load off the daily work.
Voice notes instead of typing. Meetings captured so you don't have to write them up. Conversations that hold the context of your projects, so you can pick up where you left off without re-explaining everything from scratch.
L3
Thinking partner
A second mind, available when you need one.
For ideas you've been carrying. For decisions that deserve more thought than they're getting. For working things out, out loud, with something that listens and pushes back. The work doesn't always need a person — sometimes it just needs the right kind of attention.
L4
Tools that fit
Build things shaped to your work.
Not the generic app everyone uses. A tool designed for exactly how you actually operate — your invoicing, your bookings, your particular shape of project — without needing to know how to code.
L5
Personal agents
Work that takes care of itself.
AI that observes, decides, and acts in the background. Not assisting you turn by turn — running on its own. For the recurring work that doesn't need your attention to happen, just your judgment when something unusual comes up.

The shift

Where the real shift happens.

The meaningful shift isn't between L1 and L2. It's at Level 3.

This is where AI stops being a tool you reach for and becomes a thinking partner you work with. Where the projects you've been carrying meet a second mind. Where the decisions you've been putting off finally get worked through — out loud, with something that listens and pushes back.

The first step is usually quieter than people expect. But what changes is qualitative, not incremental: not "I use AI more" — "I think differently now."

What this work usually looks like: clearing some of the clutter you've been carrying, activating one project that's been waiting, and building a real working relationship with AI as a thinking partner — not occasionally, but as part of how you actually think.

Less clutter. More presence.
