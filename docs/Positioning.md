# Positioning — Working Document

*The language, the differentiator, the headlines. For posts, pitches, the website, the conversations. Written for entrepreneurs and SME owners — EN and FR.*

**Audience:** Entrepreneurs and SME owners — solo consultants, small agencies, professional service firms, online businesses with operations, and the small organisations, associations, and communities that share the same shape. EN and FR speaking. Switzerland, France, Belgium, UK.

**Practice:** Frank Sykes • AI coaching with humanistic intake and agentic delivery

---

## The differentiator

Humanistic intake, agentic delivery. The session feels like therapy; the artefact runs like a product. Every other AI consultant is one or the other. The tactical/tech-bro people deliver infrastructure but don't see the person. The genuinely humanistic ones see the person but leave them with a feeling, not a system. You sit in a thin slot in the middle, and that's the entire game.

## What you're not

You're not a tools trainer. The shape of a tools trainer is: here's the platform, here are the buttons, here's how to prompt. Your work doesn't look like that even when it looks like that. You're walking someone through a transition — from doing the work to doing the work with a second mind — and the platform is just the place that transition happens to occur.

## What you actually sell

Three things, layered:

- **Understanding** — a humanistic diagnostic that takes the whole business and the whole person. Not what tools they want to use. What's been carried, what's been waiting, what's recurring that they're tired of doing.
- **Companionship** — six sessions where someone walks alongside the transition, instead of dropping off a course and disappearing. The thing they almost never get as a business owner.
- **A system they keep** — at the end, they own a working environment. Their Cowork install, their Skills, their routines, their Project structure. If they stop working with you, the system continues to work for them. Their proposals still draft themselves. Their Monday brief still arrives. That's the rarest thing in coaching, and it's structural here, not aspirational.

## Who you're pitching, sharpened

"Entrepreneurs and SME owners" is too broad as an outreach target. Four sharper sub-segments to actually pitch — same core positioning, use cases tuned to each:

- **Solo consultants and consultancies under 10 people** — knowledge-work practices charging for thinking. They get L3 instantly because the carrying is their daily experience.
- **Agency owners (marketing, design, communications)** — routine output (proposals, weekly client updates, content calendars) that L4 transforms. The most pain-aware buyer in the funnel.
- **Professional service firms (small accounting, tax, advisory, notarial)** — Swiss and French specifically, where language and cultural fit are real. Entrepreneurs running regulated businesses but small enough to make their own decisions.
- **Online businesses and creators with operations** — coaches, course-creators, e-commerce under €2M. Lean, leverage-seeking, comfortable buying tools. Easiest cold market.

Plus the widening: **small organisations, associations, and communities running on lean teams.** Same lived experience — the person leading is also doing the work. Disqualifiers from "Not for" still apply.

## The entrepreneur-specific edge

Everything runs through them. They're holding operations, hiring, finances, the offer they've been meaning to refine, the website they've been meaning to update, the person they keep meaning to write back to. They don't have a peer cohort. They don't have a thinking partner. They don't have a chief of staff.

The humanistic register reads as serious because it's the first time anyone has spoken to that part of their experience instead of pitching them another tool. And the agentic delivery reads as serious because they buy systems — they're already paying for software, they understand monthly subscriptions, they know what implementation looks like.

## The shift you sell, named

The meaningful move isn't from L1 to L2 — they'll get there themselves with a free ChatGPT account. It's two transitions at once: at L3, where AI stops being a tool you reach for and becomes a thinking partner you work with. And at L4, where recurring work starts producing itself in their voice.

For this audience, **L4 is the centre of gravity, not L3.** Entrepreneurs and operators want their voice in their proposals and their newsletters. They want the system to *do things*. The headline shift is both: *I think differently now* AND *the routine work gets done*.

The line that lands:

> *Not "I use AI more" — "I think differently now, and the work that used to wait on me doesn't anymore."*

## The promise, framed humanely

More room — not because you work less, but because you stop carrying what doesn't need carrying. Things move — the proposals, the follow-ups, the weekly brief. Things ship — the offer you've been meaning to write, the case study you never published, the page that's been at 80% for six months.

## The boundary that earns trust

Stopping at the right level is a success, not a plateau. Most clients should live comfortably between L3 and L4. L5 — full personal agents — is for a subset, and worth saying you'll tell them when they're ready and not before. You can name where someone shouldn't go, which is what makes recommendations credible when you do make them.

## The funnel, with launch framing

Three steps, each doing one job. The launch terms are positioning choices, not just operations — they signal seriousness and selectivity in the right places.

- **A 15-minute connect call** — free, no obligation. The qualifier. We see if there's a fit; if not, fifteen minutes spent. If yes, the next conversation is booked.
- **A 60-minute Discovery session** — the real first conversation. Sixty minutes to understand what they're trying to make more room for. A follow-up document twenty-four hours later. Two experiments to try the week after. *Preferential rate for the first 15 entrepreneurs registering, through end of June 2026 — as I launch this practice.* Reference rate: CHF 250.
- **Coaching Together** — six weekly sessions. Cowork set up, Skills shaped to their voice, routines that produce recurring work, one project they've been carrying brought visibly forward. CHF 1,200 – 1,800. *Currently accepting a small number of first-cohort clients through summer 2026.*

The scarcity signal lives on the paid engagement, not the discovery — where selectivity reads as confidence. The discovery stays low-friction by design.

## Headlines

Different surfaces, different jobs. Public-facing copy strips jargon (no "humanistic," no "agentic") — those words belong in the internal positioning and in conversations with people who've already leaned in.

### The website hero — three layers

```
H1:        AI coaching, in your voice.
Sub-head:  For entrepreneurs and small teams.
           Six weeks of working sessions. Tools that fit you.

Below:     Less weight. More room. A second mind to think with.
```

The H1 names what (AI coaching) and how it's different (in your voice) — no jargon. The sub-head names audience and shape concretely (six weeks, tools that fit). The under-hero callout makes the promise across L2, L4, and L3 in nine plain words.

### The dinner-party line — for conversations and DMs

> *AI for entrepreneurs who don't want to sound like a tech bro.*

### The post signature — for LinkedIn and social

> *AI made easy, by me.*

Personal, first-person, soft promise. Works as a sign-off or a header on posts in your voice. Not a brand headline — a voice marker.

### The long-form opener — for pitches, emails, structural arguments

> *Most AI coaches teach you to use the tools. I help you shape a system that runs your operation — and grows every time we meet.*

Too dense for a website hero (the experience of yesterday) — but the right opening sentence for a pitch deck, a cold email, or the spoken differentiator when someone asks what makes you different. Pairs well with the structural argument further down.

## What to lead with in posts and pitches

A short content stance worth running this week. Four beats, in this order:

- **Lead with the carrying** — the offer they've been meaning to write, the SOPs that exist only in their head, the case study that's been at 80% for months. Almost no one in your space talks to this.
- **Then the clearing** — the friction they don't notice they're carrying. Re-drafting proposals. Re-explaining the business to every freelancer. Re-prepping the same kind of meeting. Frame it as weight, not time.
- **Then the doing** — the recurring work that stops waiting on them. The weekly brief, the proposal draft, the follow-up sequence. Framed as relief, not automation.
- **Then the shift** — what L3 actually feels like from the inside. Thinking out loud about a difficult hire and having something useful come back. Working through pricing and feeling the decision settle.

## Vocabulary

**Words to avoid**

Productivity. Automation (unless being specific). AI-native. 10x. Unlock. Transform. Empower. Leverage as a verb. Humanistic and agentic (on public-facing copy — keep them internal). System and operation (in a hero context — fine elsewhere). These filter for the wrong audience and signal you sound like everyone else.

**Words that do work**

Room. Carrying. Waiting. Weight. Ships. Runs. Thinks with you. Fits you. Voice. Your operation (in a body paragraph, not a hero). Less. More. Make sense. Calmly.

## The structural argument, when you need it

When a prospect asks what makes you different from the dozen other AI consultants in their inbox, the spoken answer in three beats:

- Most AI training teaches the platform. I teach the transition — from doing the work to doing the work with a second mind. That's a different conversation.
- You leave with a system you own. Your Skills, your routines, your Project structure. If we stop working together, your proposals still draft themselves. Your Monday brief still arrives. Most coaches leave you with notes.
- And it compounds. Every session adds to the system. Six months in, your system is shaped to how you actually work, not how a generic AI thinks you should work. That's the part that's hard to copy.

Doesn't need to appear on a page. But when someone asks, you've got the answer in three sentences.

## The five levels are diagnostic, not marketing

The L1–L5 framework is revealed inside paid sessions. It does not appear on the website, in posts, or in cold outreach. It's the diagnostic instrument that makes the discovery session feel earned — putting it on the homepage trades the most valuable conversational asset for a piece of marketing copy that doesn't convert anyway. Hold the line.
